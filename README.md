# PlanetVote — The Planet's Vote

**A framework for planetary-scale direct democracy**

> *"When will we vote as individuals for things that affect us as a world?"*
> — Paul Innes, *Make or Break: The Extraordinary Life of Paul Innes* (2022)

---

## What is PlanetVote?

PlanetVote is a proposed global direct-democracy platform enabling every adult human being on Earth to vote on planetary-scale issues — the decisions that are genuinely indifferent to national borders.

Asteroid impact. Supervolcanic eruption. Pandemic preparedness. AI governance. Climate response. These are decisions that affect every person alive, yet no mechanism exists for the people of Earth to express a collective democratic mandate on any of them. The United Nations represents governments, not people. Its Security Council can be vetoed by any one of five powers chosen by the outcome of a war that ended 81 years ago.

PlanetVote proposes to fix this.

---

## Core principles

* **One person, one vote** — every adult human being has exactly equal voting weight regardless of nationality, wealth, or geography
* **Supermajority legitimacy** — no resolution passes without 66.7% of participating voters; crisis votes require 75%; constitutional changes require 80%
* **Lunar cycle voting** — seven issues per 29.5-day cycle, selected by a transparent, auditable algorithm
* **Emergency override** — planetary-class threats trigger a crisis vote within six hours of verified confirmation, bypassing the standard cycle
* **Radical transparency** — every line of code, every algorithmic parameter, every vote count is publicly auditable in real time
* **Cryptographic privacy** — zero-knowledge proofs guarantee that individual votes cannot be identified or coerced
* **Subsidiarity** — PlanetVote votes only on issues that genuinely require planetary coordination; all other matters remain with nations and communities

## How it works

### The voting cycle

PlanetVote organises standard voting around the lunar cycle — approximately 29.5 days. Each cycle, a trending algorithm selects seven issues for the global ballot. Voters may cast their ballot at any point during the cycle. Results are published at cycle close. Passed resolutions are transmitted to the World Government Council for implementation coordination.

### The algorithm

Issues are surfaced by signal velocity, geographic diversity across continental regions, scientific consensus weighting, urgency decay, and recency penalties. The algorithm's source code, parameters, and output logs are published in real time on a public ledger.

### Emergency override protocol

For threats of planetary-class urgency, the standard cycle is suspended. A crisis vote opens within six hours of verified threat confirmation by at least three internationally recognised scientific institutions. Crisis votes remain open for 48 hours with a 75% supermajority threshold.

### World Government Council

Seventeen Regional Representatives plus a Secretary-General, all elected by supermajority, serve as the executive layer translating passed resolutions into coordinated international action. The Council has no authority beyond the mandates passed to it by the voting population.

## Technical architecture

PlanetVote is built on five pillars:

1. **Self-sovereign identity** — biometric zero-knowledge credentials using one-way hashes, anchored to decentralised identifiers on a public blockchain. No central identity registry.
2. **Zero-knowledge voting** — homomorphic encryption allows votes to be tallied without decrypting individual ballots; range proofs allow individual verification without identification
3. **Distributed infrastructure** — hosted across 12+ jurisdictions; no single nation can shut it down
4. **Open-source algorithm** — fully auditable trending system with a 30-day notice period for any parameter changes
5. **Blockchain confirmation** — every vote cryptographically verified and permanently recorded

## The founding story

PlanetVote was conceived by Paul Innes, a disability rights advocate and community founder based in Cairns, Far North Queensland, Australia. Paul has lived with quadriplegia for 38 years following a spinal injury at age 20. In that time he founded Independence World (a disability care service), established the site of Australia's first Rainbow Gathering, and advocated publicly for human rights.

The core idea appeared in his 2022 autobiography: *"If the United Nations were a body where not just nations could vote but every individual on the planet, we might start to see the truth of how all the people of the world really think."*

PlanetVote is not a sudden inspiration. It is the culmination of nearly four decades of thinking about justice, community, and what a genuinely fair world would require.
